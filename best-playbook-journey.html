BEST Team Playbook

Delivery Lifecycle

A journey from discovery to monitoring — 9 phases · 4 stages
Key Principle Sign-off is targeted but not a blocker. Once the team has enough understanding, story creation runs in parallel with finalising sign-off.
Stage 1

Plan

Align with stakeholders on what to build. Stories begin in parallel.

1
Discovery
Calls, mockups, whiteboards, shared sheets. Aim for a requirements meeting and stakeholder sign-off — but don't wait for it.
/best-mockup
2
Spike + Story creation
Fix version defined. Spikes and user stories created and refined. RKD created in Confluence and updated continuously.
/create-spike /create-user-story /release-kickoff /update-rkd
Stage 2

Build

Plan effort and write tests in parallel with engineering work.

3
Estimation
Release owner runs an estimation meeting using a shared sheet to collect estimates from team members.
/estimation
4
Test scenarios
E2E scenarios written in parallel with engineering. Output goes to a new tab in the BT E2E Master List sheet.
/create-test-scenarios /regression-tests
Stage 3

Validate

Walk through internally, then expose to PM and stakeholders.

5
Pre-E2E (two parts)
5a — Engineering team: internal walkthrough.
5b — PM (Christine): review before stakeholders.
no skill
6
UAT preparation
UAT Confluence page from the test sheet, rewritten for the stakeholder audience.
/create-uat
7
E2E with stakeholder
Live walkthrough with stakeholders, then UAT page handed over for them to execute and sign off.
no skill
Stage 4

Deliver & Monitor

Ship to production and watch how it performs.

8
Release
After UAT sign-offs, release goes to production with release notes sent ahead. Internal and stakeholder Slack drafts.
/create-release-notes /release-announcement
9
Post-release monitoring
Feature monitored on production with a report and SLO tracking. Bugs flow into the BAU PSR triage process.
no skill
↻ Cycle continues — next release feeds back into Plan ↻

Monthly
